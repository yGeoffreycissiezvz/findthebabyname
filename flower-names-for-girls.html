Flower names for girls

Floral names are perennially loved—pretty, meaningful, and full of personality. They range from timeless (Rose, Lily) to rare and romantic (Dahlia, Marigold), with a meaning blooming behind each one.

Advertisement

Why flower names never fade

They are instantly recognizable, easy to spell, and carry gentle symbolism—Rose for love, Lily for purity, Iris for wisdom. Floral names also work across styles: as a soft first name, a sweet middle, or a charming half of a double name.

Classic flower names

RoseLove; perfect first or middle
LilyPurity; light and pretty
VioletModesty; vintage-cool
IrisWisdom + rainbow goddess
DaisyInnocence; cheerful
JasmineGrace; fragrant

Rare & romantic flower names

DahliaElegance; striking
MarigoldWarmth; vintage-rare
MagnoliaDignity; Southern charm
AzaleaPassion; vivid
LinneaTwinflower; Scandinavian
ZinniaEndurance; spunky

Subtle flower names

Some floral names do not shout “flower,” which appeals to parents who want softness without obviousness: Susan/Susanna (lily), Leila (night-blooming lily associations), Fleur (French for flower), Flora (Roman goddess of flowers), and Yara (often linked to flowers/butterfly).

Flower names in double names & nature themes

Floral firsts pair beautifully in the record-high double-name trend—think Lily Mae or Rose Eleanor. They also sit within the broader nature name movement, alongside tree and sky names.

Choosing a flower name

  1. Consider the symbolism. Each flower has a traditional meaning—pick one that resonates.
  2. Check popularity. Lily and Violet rank high; Marigold and Zinnia stay rare.
  3. Test as first vs. middle. Some bloom best up front, others as a soft middle.
  4. Say it with the surname. Avoid floral first + plant-y surname unless intentional.
